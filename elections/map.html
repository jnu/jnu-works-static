Vampires vs. Werewolves

For whatever reason, Werewolves tend to be more popular in rural areas. Not popular enough, though: Vampires carry the day.

Mac vs. PC

You may be incredulous as you look around you in the coffeeshop and calculate that everyone has a Mac except that one poor dork in the corner slapping his PC and muttering obscenities under his breath. But PCs still outnumber Macs more than 15 to 1.

Cats vs. Dogs

If cats and dogs in the US were fighting (or just playing) to put one of their own in the White House, the cat candidate would be elected by a wide margin. Of course, this assumes a ruff-ly equal voter turn-out, and it's never wise to assume a thing about a cat.

Latkes vs. Hamentashn

Finally an issue that really matters, and half the country doesn't even care. Hamentashn are the pretty clear winner here.

Pie vs. Cake

America elects cake in a landslide, or rather a delicious chocolately mudslide. As a pie-lover I'm a little disappointed. But I suppose I can live with cake.

Pancakes vs. Waffles

Waffles defeat the stodgy Northerners and their pancakes. Vice-president, naturally, is Chicken.

Squares vs. Circles

Notice how New England prefers squares to circles, yet pancakes to waffles.

Pizza Hut vs. Taco Bell

Taco Bell would become our first Tex-Mexican president. But thankfully we don't actually have to choose.

Good vs. Evil

Lastly, I thought it would be useful to resolve a fundamental philosophical feud, Good versus Evil. It turns out to be a very close race, but, as you might expect, Evil prevails. That does not bode well for tomorrow!
